How an international investor actually plugs in

1. Set up (or use) a U.S. affiliate company

We usually form a Delaware LLC that you own.
• Keeps U.S. bookkeeping separate from your home‑country books.
• Helps manage FIRPTA (the special U.S. withholding on real‑estate gains).

2. Choose your “booster”

Step a
OZ route

Your affiliate sells an asset inside or outside the U.S. and shows a capital gain that is subject to U.S. tax.

LIHTC route

We bid for a slice of LIHTC credits in a project already awarded by the state housing agency.

Step b
OZ route

Within 180 days it wires the gain into a Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF).

LIHTC route

Your affiliate buys a limited‑partner stake; credits pass through to its tax return each year.

Step c
OZ route

We draft the legal docs, file the QOF registration and monitor the 90 % asset test each June and December.

LIHTC route

Our CPA files Form 8609 and yearly compliance reports so the credits stay safe.

3. Enjoy the benefits — without the paperwork headache

Our in‑house CPA + real‑estate attorney do the heavy lifting:

• Quarterly statements in your currency and English.
• IRS, state, and lender filings handled on time.
• Treaty analysis so you don’t pay the same tax twice back home.
